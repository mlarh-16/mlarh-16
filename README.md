## Mohamed Khalil Larhrib

**Policy Officer at [The ONE Campaign](https://www.one.org/ca/).** Co-author of [*Canada-Africa Strategy at One Year: Turning Words into Action*](https://www.one.org/ca/what-we-do/the-issues/canada-africa-strategy-words-progress-action-implementation/) (with Elise Legault and Ciuriak Consulting, March 2026).

I work at the intersection of policy and data. MA in International Relations (St John's University, Rome). Previously based at the Permanent Representation of Morocco to the United Nations in Rome, working on food security and African development policy.

I produce policy analysis and data pipelines on Africa-focused international affairs — published with senior trade economists, on rapid-response political files, and in peer-researcher collaborations.

Based in Ottawa, Canada. Open to Canada-based and remote-global roles.

### Selected writing

- **[Canada-Africa Strategy at One Year: Turning Words into Action](https://www.one.org/ca/what-we-do/the-issues/canada-africa-strategy-words-progress-action-implementation/)** — The ONE Campaign, March 2026. Co-authored with Elise Legault and Ciuriak Consulting. BACI-based trade projections to 2035 across manufactured goods, agri-food, and mined products. *My role: background research, first-draft writing, data visualizations, and applied data analysis building on Ciuriak Consulting's trade model; coordinated reviews with internal stakeholders at ONE.*
- **[Why Canada Should Trade More With Africa](https://data.one.org/analysis/why-canada-should-trade-more-with-africa)** — ONE Data, 2025. Data analysis and visualizations on Canada's untapped trade potential with African countries.
- **[Canada's G7 Presidency and Africa's Financing Crisis](https://www.one.org/ca/stories/g7-2025/)** — The ONE Campaign, June 2025. Co-authored with Elise Legault. Cost-of-capital gap and energy financing in sub-Saharan Africa, drawing on World Bank, UNCTAD, IEA, CGD, and Brookings sources.
- **[Election Update: Political Platforms](https://www.one.org/ca/stories/political-platforms/)** — The ONE Campaign, April 2025. *Sole author.* Comparative analysis of Liberal, Conservative, NDP, and Bloc Québécois federal platforms on Africa engagement, trade diversification, and foreign aid ahead of the 2025 Canadian federal election.
- **[Future-Proofing Women's Work: Toward a Gender-Responsive Just Transition in Bangladesh's RMG Sector](https://www.bacharlorai.com/research/residency/2025-26-policy-brief-rmg-fashion)** — BacharLorai Policy Residency, 2026. Co-authored with Abyaaz Khan (MPP, University of Toronto). Occupational health, gender, and climate adaptation.
